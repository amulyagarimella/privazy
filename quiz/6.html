Part 2: Personalization Nation

Many companies make money by selling your personal data to advertisers (in 2019, Facebook made $56.5B from selling users' personal data). As you probably know, these advertisers use your information age, gender, race, political leaning, health info... to figure out what ads to show you.

Let's take mental health as an example.Let's take mental health as an example: advertisers can see whether you've searched for mental health services online.

Sometimes, that can be used for good. Sometimes, that can be used for good — for instance, researchers can target ads promoting free mental health services to people who've looked for mental health support.

But not everyone has our best interests at heart. But not everyone has our best interests at heart. In May 2022, the Mozilla Foundation found that personal data (like name, phone number, and email) from therapy apps like BetterHelp and Talkspace can be accessed by third-party advertisers. Any of these third parties could know, in other words, whether you're using a therapy, and even how often you talk with a therapist.
