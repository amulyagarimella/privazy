Part 3: What Next?

You've learned a lot about digital privacy today.

What can apps track about us? Pretty much everything we do, from our location to our all of our Internet activities.

Why do they track all of this information? Apps can sell this information to advertisers, making billions off of you. Advertisers love having more information to target their ads and make even more money.

Why does it matter? Apps and advertisers without your best interests at heart can learn and exploit all kinds of information about you, even really personal information.

No matter what, we think privacy is really important. It's what stops the rest of the world from knowing the things we'd rather keep to ourselves. In fact, it's a fundamental human right.

Personally, we like to remember this saying: "If the service is free, you're the product." Apps and advertisers don't always have our best interests at heart, and that's why we encourage you to think about ways to protect your digital privacy, in a way that fits your life.
