Part 3: What Next?

Ultimately, it's your decision how to move forward. For you, deleting TikTok might be worth it to avoid those creepy invasive feelings. On the other hand, TikTok might be a lot of fun and a great way to connect with friends!

Here are some things we encourage everyone to do:

Use privacy-protecting browser extensions. Use privacy-protecting browser extensions like Disconnect, made by the company that performed the TikTok investigation. Disconnect shows you how websites are trying to track you and blocks a lot of that data collection. Privacy experts often recommend uBlock Origin, as well, which stops all kinds of online trackers.

Change your browser's privacy settings. Change your browser's privacy settings. A lot of browsers have built-in controls you can use to block trackers, including cookies, pixels, and other technologies. Open your browser's preferences or settings, and you'll usually find the controls in the privacy section.

Try a more private browser. Try a more private browser. Google Chrome collects a lot of data on behalf of Google. The Consumer Reports Security Planner recommends Firefox and Brave as more privacy-focused options.
